  • dais ˈdāəs
    a low platform for a lectern, seats of honor, or a throne
  • lectern 讲台
  • steward ˈst(y)o͞oərd
    1. a person who looks after the passengers on a ship, aircraft, or train and brings them meals.
    2. an official appointed to supervise arrangements or keep order at a large public event, for example a sporting event.
    3. a person employed to manage another’s property, especially a large house or estate.
  • porter 棒棒😋
    a person employed to carry luggage and other loads, especially in a railroad station, airport, or hotel.
  • decant
    gradually pour (liquid, typically wine or a solution) from one container into another, especially without disturbing the sediment
  • decanter
    a stoppered glass container into which wine is decanted.
  • sediment 潭滓，沉淀
    matter that settles to the bottom of a liquid; dregs
  • stopper 塞子
    1. a plug for sealing a hole, especially in the neck of a bottle or other container.
    2. a person or thing that halts or obstructs a specified thing: [ in combination ] : a crime-stopper.

    an unfermented drink made by crushing fruit, typically apples.

  • ferment 发酵； fermentation, unfermented, fermented

28th

  • conform conform to; conform with
    1. comply with rules, standards, or laws: 遵从
    2. (of a person) behave according to socially acceptable conventions or standards 遵守
    3. be similar in form or type; 符合
  • comply comply with
    1. (of a person or group) act in accordance with a wish or command
    2. (of an article) meet specified standards
  • inset 嵌入
    a thing that is put in or inserted: a pair of doors with their original stained-glass insets.
  • flutter
    (of a bird or other winged creature) fly unsteadily or hover by flapping the wings quickly and lightly
    flutter to move or fall with a light irregular or trembling motion: the remaining petals fluttered to the ground.
    flutter forward to (of a person) move restlessly or uncertainly
    of a pulse or heartbeat, beat feebly or irregularly.
  • petal ˈpedl 花瓣
    each of the segments of the corolla of a flower, which are modified leaves and are typically colored
  • corolla
    the petals of a flower 花冠
  • sepal ˈsēpəl (一片)花萼片
    calyx ˈkāliks 花萼
  • flicker
    light or a source of light shine unsteadily; vary rapidly in brightness
    a flame burn fitfully, alternately flaring up and dying down
    闪现：amusement flickered briefly in his eyes.
    make small, quick movements; flutter rapidly: in the flicker of an eyelid 一眨眼的工夫
    (of someone’s eyes) move quickly in a particular direction in order to look at something: 瞥一眼
  • fitful fitfully 断断续续
    active or occurring spasmodically or intermittently; not regular or steady
  • staleness 过期
    (of food) no longer fresh and pleasant to eat; hard, musty, or dry
    no longer new and interesting or exciting
    get stale (of a person) no longer able to perform well or creatively because of having done something for too long
    (of a check or legal claim) invalid because out of date.

29th

  • e.g. exempli gratia == for example
  • i.e. id est == in other words
  • etc. et cetera == and so on
  • et al. et alibi == and elsewhere et alii == and others
  • scarf
    a length or square of fabric worn around the neck or head.

  • delivery

    1. the action of delivering letters, packages, or ordered goods 发送
    2. an item or items delivered on a particular occasion 包裹
    3. Law the formal or symbolic handing over of property, especially a sealed deed, to a grantee or third party. 正式移交
    4. the process of giving birth 分娩
    5. the manner or style of giving a speech 说话风格 one’s delivery is strange
    6. the supply or provision of something: delivery of electricity at a specified price.

    1. catch up with and pass while traveling in the same direction
    2. become greater or more successful than
    3. (especially of misfortune) come suddenly or unexpectedly upon: the pattern of economic ruin overtook them.
    4. (of a feeling) affect (someone) suddenly and powerfully: weariness overtook him and he retired to bed.

  • coarse 粗糙

    1. rough or loose in texture or grain:
      • made of large grains or particles: dry, coarse sand.
      • (of grains or particles) large.
      • (of a person’s features) not elegantly formed or proportioned.
      • (of food or drink) of inferior quality.
    2. (of a person or their speech) rude, crude, or vulgar.

    nearness in space, time, or relationship
